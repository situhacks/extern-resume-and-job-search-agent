### opener-observation — open on a real read of the firm, not a template
BASE: I am writing to express my strong interest in the Business Analyst role at Meridian & Co.
YOU:  The slides that survive a consulting engagement are the ones someone owned all the way through, and that ownership is the part of your analyst role that made me apply.
MOVE: Open on a specific observation about how *this* firm works, framed as why it drew me — not a template line a recruiter has read a thousand times.

### proof-one-story — one owned, quantified story beats a list of traits
BASE: I have strong analytical skills, attention to detail, and experience with market analysis.

MOVE: Replace the trait list with one story I owned, with a concrete outcome and a detail that proves it was real. Show, don't assert.

### ai-honesty — name how I actually use AI, plainly, as judgment not hype
BASE: I am highly proficient with AI tools and leverage them to drive efficiency and innovation.
YOU:  I try to use AI the way a good analyst should — a tool I check, not one I trust blindly; I built a workflow that makes the model show its sources and challenge its own logic before I believe a number.
MOVE: Drop the buzzword flex. Describe the judgment, not the tool — the thing firms actually want to hear on AI.

### closer-direct — a short, warm, confident ask
BASE: I would be thrilled to have the opportunity to discuss how I would be a great fit for your team.
YOU:  I'd be glad to bring that to Meridian's operations work. Happy to chat whenever works.
MOVE: Close with a direct, warm ask. No "great fit," no begging — confidence the work has earned.
