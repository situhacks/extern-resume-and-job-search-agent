# Taste interview — voice intake

How to pull a voice profile out of someone applying for jobs. Run this **last**, after mining their writing —
it only fills what the samples couldn't. Save raw answers to `transcript-{date}.md`, then compile into
`../voice-profile.md` and seed `../pairs/`.

## The ordering rule
Mine existing writing FIRST (past cover letters, emails, essays, dictated notes — richer and cheaper than
self-report). Then ask only the gaps.
- **Cold-start** (nothing written): the full set below, ~45–60 min.
- **Gap-fill** (some writing exists): mine it first, then ask only what's missing — usually ~15 questions, ~15 min.

## Mechanics
A conversation, not a form. Have them **dictate** — speech captures their real pattern before the internal editor
sanitizes it. Push on vague answers — "I write professionally" is useless; demand a concrete example of
professional-done-well vs. stiff-and-generic. Never fill a vague answer with a guess.

## What to ask (depth over coverage; skip what the samples answered)
1. **The cringe test** — what specific move in a cover letter or application makes you wince? Not "it's generic"
   — the actual phrase or structure. (Highest signal. Start here.)
2. **What you admire** — whose cover letter / writing made you think "yes, like that," and what was it doing?
   (Gives the reference anchor.)
3. **Mechanics** — sentence length, punctuation habits, how you naturally talk through a story out loud.
4. **What you actually believe** — about consulting, the work, why you want it. The conviction under the
   application, not the talking point.
5. **Personality** — how warm vs. formal you go; any humour; how you sound in a real email to someone you respect
   vs. a formal letter.
6. **Hard nos** — words, framings, tones you refuse (the begging, the buzzwords, the fake enthusiasm).
7. **How you work** — do you dictate? edit by hand? where does your voice actually enter? (Shapes how the agent
   hands you drafts.)

## Compile
Write the dense profile to `../voice-profile.md` (their words, not adjectives). Turn any concrete "I'd write it
THIS way not THAT way" moments into pairs in `../pairs/`. The cringe answers become anti-patterns; the
admired-writing answer becomes the reference anchor.
