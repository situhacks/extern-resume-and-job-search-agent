# Food-delivery market entry

**S — Situation:** A regional food-delivery startup wasn't sure whether to expand into two adjacent cities.
**T — Task:** I owned the market-sizing and competitor workstream on our student-consulting team.
**A — Action:**
- Built a bottoms-up demand model (population → addressable households → order frequency → share) instead of a top-down guess.
- Ran 10 customer and operator interviews to ground the assumptions and pressure-test the model.
- Built the one slide the recommendation rested on, and defended the number when our advisor pushed on it.
**R — Result:** The client adopted our recommendation to enter one city and hold on the other; the model became their planning baseline.
**R — Reflection:** Building the number from first principles (not borrowing one) is what made it survive scrutiny — that's the lesson I'd carry onto a real engagement.
