# Cover letter architecture — HCPA (2026 paradigm)

A modern cover letter is SHORT and asymmetrical — 4 tight moves, not 5 fat paragraphs. The 400-500 word letter
is dead. So is the "6-month plan" inside the letter (that goes in interviews, not here).

## Length ceilings (hard — shorter is stronger)

| Target | Word count | Absolute max |
|---|---|---|
| Tier-1 consulting (MBB) | 150–180 | 200 |
| Big 4 / Accenture / boutique | 150–180 | 200 |
| Startup / smaller firm | 120–160 | 180 |

Length scales **inversely** with how senior/competitive the role is — more prestige, more brevity. If you're
writing a Month-1/Month-2 plan inside the letter, stop: you're 3x over budget.

## The four moves

1. **H — Hook (~30–50 words):** a HYPERSPECIFIC observation about THIS firm that an LLM couldn't hallucinate —
   a recent engagement/practice they're growing, a partner's stated POV, something real about *them*.
   **This requires the company research** (`research-brief-v1.md` in the application folder). NOT a truism about consulting
   ("the best slides are owned end to end"), NOT praise ("I admire your firm"), NOT a JD recital. If you have
   no real specific about the firm, you cannot write a real hook — get the research brief first.
2. **C — Connection (~40–60 words):** ONE granular past experience mapped to ONE thing this firm does. A single
   deep thread, not a tour of every job.
3. **P — Proof (~20–40 words):** ONE quantified outcome, in a sentence. Give the reader one concrete thing to remember.
4. **A — Ask (~10–25 words):** a clean, direct ask for the conversation. No groveling, no meta-narrative.

## Hard rules
- Open with the Hook. Never "I am writing to express my interest in...".
- No buzzwords: leverage, synergies, innovative, dynamic, passionate about.
- No "I'd be a great fit" / generic enthusiasm. Show the work; let it speak.
- Cut anything that doesn't hook, connect, prove, or ask.
